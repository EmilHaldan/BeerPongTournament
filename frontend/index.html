          Game Settings

          Rules

          The Setup

          • 6 cups per side, arranged in a triangle rack
          • Cups are filled with beer (or water if preferred)
          • Tables should be roughly 8 feet long

          Gameplay

          • Each team takes turns throwing 2 balls per turn (one per player)
          • If a ball lands in a cup, that cup is removed and the opposing team drinks it
          • Bounce shots count for 2 cups — but the defending team may swat them out of the air
          • If both players on a team hit cups on the same turn, they get the balls back for one extra throw ("balls back")

          Re-racks

          • The defending team may request a re-rack when 6, 3, 2, or 1 cups remain
          • One re-rack per game per team unless house rules say otherwise

          Island

          • A player may "call an island" on an isolated cup (not touching any other cup)
          • If the called cup is hit, it counts as 2 cups
          • If a different cup is hit instead, it does NOT count

          Winning

          • First team to eliminate all the opponent's cups wins — subject to redemption
          • Redemption: after losing the last cup, the losing team gets one final turn. If they clear the remaining cups, the game goes to sudden-death with 3 cups each side

          Fouls

          • Elbows must stay behind the edge of the table when throwing
          • Interfering with a ball in flight (other than swatting a bounce) is a foul — the opposing team may remove one of your cups

          Tournament Format

          • Matches are organised into "heats" — a batch of parallel games
          • Each match is a single game, no best-of
          • Leaderboard tracks wins and total cups hit
          • Ties are recorded but count as neither a win nor a loss
